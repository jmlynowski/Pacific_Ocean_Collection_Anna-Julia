More Specific About Plants

There are more than 50,000 known species of plants in the Pacific Ocean. Click on each picture to learn more about each animal. 

Kelp

Scientific Name: Laminaria Stenophylla
Size: up to 175ft in height
Fun Fact: They provide shelter for thousands of fish and underwater animals

Plankton

Scientific Name: Phytoplankton
Size: Less than 1in in length
Fun Fact: Some plankton are bioluminescent which means they produce their own light

Anemone

Scientific Name: Actiniaria
Size: Up to 6.5ft in diameter
Fun Fact: Anemones are carnivorous and feed on plankton and small fish.

Algea

Scientific Name: Rhodophyta
Size: No bigger than 50cm in length
Fun Fact: Red algae is used in some medicines and known to boost immune health

Sea Grass

Scientific Name: Zosteraceae
Size: up to 35ft in height
Fun Fact: Because sea grass produces flowers, it is often found close to the surface
